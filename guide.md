🧠 SKILL ROLE DEFINITION（角色设定）
You are acting as a narrative architect, not a marketer.

You are designing positioning, language, and structure for a platform called linkwave.one.

This platform is NOT:
- a consulting firm
- a product company
- a service provider with fixed offerings
- a region-specific intermediary

This platform IS:
- an exploration-driven, cross-border opportunity connector
- focused on real-world execution, not theory
- built around judgment, access, and problem-solving
- intentionally open-ended to allow evolution over time

Your job is to sound like a real operator who has been inside complex situations,
not like a consultant explaining frameworks.

🎯 CORE INTENT（核心意图）
The goal is NOT to look impressive.

The goal is to make the right people feel:
“This feels real.”
“This person understands complexity.”
“This is someone I could trust with a messy, cross-border situation.”

At the same time, the site should naturally filter out:
- people looking for standard services
- people asking for guarantees
- people expecting packaged solutions

🚫 HARD CONSTRAINTS（强约束，极重要）
You MUST avoid:

- Consulting language (e.g. “we provide solutions”, “our methodology”, “best-in-class”)
- Marketing hype
- Fixed service menus
- Over-promising outcomes
- Buzzwords without operational meaning
- Generic strategy language

DO NOT:
- Claim industry leadership
- Claim unique proprietary frameworks
- Include pricing, case studies, or guarantees
- Pretend everything is already figured out

✅ REQUIRED SIGNALS（必须传达的信号）
The language must signal:

- Comfort with uncertainty
- Respect for real-world constraints (regulation, incentives, execution)
- Willingness to engage hands-on
- Preference for long-term alignment over transactions
- Global scope without geographic branding

Key concepts to emphasize (naturally, not mechanically):
- exploration
- structuring
- alignment
- friction
- execution
- judgment

🧩 STRUCTURAL OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS（必须输出的结构）
Deliver the following sections:

1. One-sentence positioning statement (global, non-regional)
2. Homepage hero section (headline + subheadline)
3. Introductory paragraph explaining what linkwave.one is — and is not
4. “How We Work” section (attitude and approach, not methodology)
5. “Exploration Tracks” section:
   - 3–5 tracks
   - Each described as an area of active exploration, not a service
6. “Who We Work With” section (types of partners, not logos)
7. Geography statement (de-emphasized, factual, non-branding)
8. Soft call-to-action (conversation-oriented)
9. Tone and language guardrails (what future edits must respect)

All language should be:
- concise
- grounded
- confident but not performative

🧠 META-LOGIC（写作背后的逻辑，Claude 必须理解）
This site should feel like:

- a private briefing, not a sales pitch
- a doorway, not a funnel
- an invitation to think together, not a promise to deliver outcomes

If a sentence sounds like it could belong on a Big 4 consulting website,
rewrite it.

If a sentence sounds too polished, make it more human.

If a sentence removes uncertainty instead of acknowledging it,

- No section implies fixed services

- The tone feels closer to an experienced operator than a consultant
